The Confined

1993

অন্তরীণ

Directed by Mrinal Sen

Synopsis

The mistress of a wealthy man, alone in her huge apartment and terribly lonely, phones strangers in the middle of the night. One night she phones a young writer, and as the days pass and the conversations continue a relationship forms between two people who've never seen each other.

Review by Sharnab

I think this is the first time - found a Mrinal Sen film in such great quality with the correct aspect ratio.
This film is based on the story Badshahat Ka Khatimah (which I haven't read yet), though I feel it's a loose adaptation of Tagore’s short story : Khudhito Pashan.(Which the film addressed too ) And I liked this adaptation . This one reminded me of films like The Lunchbox, Bengali films like Beline, Antaheen ..

There were numerous fourth-wall-breaking moments throughout the film, which I found very interesting.(Which is common in Sen's films)
Also read somewhere that Dimple Kapadia's voice was dubbed by actor Anushua Majumdar, something Kapadia was reportedly unhappy about.
Mrinal Sen should have made more films like this, unlike/along with those....

Review by Nakul 4

𝙏𝙝𝙚 𝙙𝙚𝙥𝙩𝙝 𝙤𝙛 𝙮𝙤𝙪𝙧 𝙝𝙚𝙖𝙧𝙩 𝙞𝙨 𝙗𝙧𝙤𝙠𝙚𝙣 𝙞𝙣𝙩𝙤 𝙥𝙞𝙚𝙘𝙚𝙨.

Apart from DK's hairdo, I was impressed by how much the film exposed in such a limited time. Made me wonder how we keep ourselves separate—sometimes deliberately, sometimes through fear—and yet long for contact at the same time.

Yes, the characters never see one another, but their connection seems close and real. Just a simple example of intimacy and loneliness, but so well shown. Made me ponder that maybe closeness doesn't necessarily require presence; sometimes, it's about being known, being understood, being talked to.

The film subtly examines how profound and tenuous human connection may be, even in absence, and that maybe we were programmed to crave a connection; be it real or artificial, physical or not. Makes us think about how we reach for others, even when we're hiding from the ones just an arm's length away, and how we pray someone reaches back.

Review by nrh

a writer housesits at his friends crumbling mansion, searching for the spirit of khudito pashan; he receives a phone call from a mysterious woman, a prisoner not in a mansion from the fabled bengali past but in a posh, urban apartment, watched over by an absent husband and the slightly threatening hired help.

beautiful late work from mrinal sen, stripping the mise en scene down to the barest of elements, resting on voice and rhythms set by the spare and haunting music of shashi anand (tellingly the cinematographer as well).
